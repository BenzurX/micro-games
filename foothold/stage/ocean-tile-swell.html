Per-tile ocean swell - timing variants

All four boards use the exact same wave graphic (the two crossing foam bands you liked), applied to each ocean tile individually - no board-wide mask, nothing animates in unison. The only thing that changes between variants is HOW each tile's start time and speed relate to its neighbours. Negative delays start every tile mid-cycle, so no variant ever "boots up" together.

1

Diagonal cascade

Each tile lags its up/left neighbour by a fixed beat (offset by row+column), with a small per-tile speed wobble. The swell reads as one wave marching diagonally across the water, tile by tile - coordinated, but never simultaneous.

2

Loose chop

Every tile gets a fully random start point and its own random speed for each band. No two tiles ever agree - the most natural "open water" feel, at the cost of any visible wave direction.

3

Traveling swell

Uniform speed everywhere, but the start time slides with the tile's column (plus a touch of row). One crest visibly sweeps across the whole ocean left to right - the closest to the old board-wide sweep, rebuilt from individual tiles.

4

Counter-swell

The bright band cascades down one diagonal while the faint band cascades the opposite way, each with its own jitter - two wave trains crossing through each other, so the water churns without ever syncing.
