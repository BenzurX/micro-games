Ocean tile shimmer — 5 treatments

Large, readable swatches (the in-game tile is much smaller) for the flat OCEAN_COLOR fill on permanent deep-water tiles. All CSS-only, no build step, matching the palette already in GameScene.js/ocean-tides.html (--foam: #bfe9f0 highlight over a --ocean: #123a4a base). A gold node dot is shown on one swatch so you can judge readability against a claimable resource sitting on top.

1

Drifting Bands

Soft diagonal light bands slide sideways at a slow, steady pace - calm, ambient "moving water" texture. Cheapest of the five (one gradient, one animation).

2

Twinkle Sparkle

Scattered points of light fade in and out on independent timers, like sun glints on a rippled surface. Busiest of the five - reads as "alive" water, but competes more with a node sitting on top.

3

Wave Crest Sweep

A single soft, blurred line sweeps top to bottom on a slow loop - reads as a gentle current or a beam of light passing through the depths. Very calm; easy to miss if you're not looking directly at it.

4

Caustic Ripple

Overlapping soft blobs of light drift independently and screen-blend together - the classic "underwater light caustics" look. Most organic and premium-feeling of the five, costs two blended layers instead of one.

5

Rolling Swell

A wide, soft band of brightness rolls back and forth like a slow swell or current moving beneath the surface. Reads as more "deep and heavy" than the others - good fit for permanent open ocean specifically, vs. the lighter shoreline fringe.

Round 2 — Rolling Swell iterations

You liked #5 the most and want it to feel more like actual waves/tides. Five pushes on the same base band, each adding one specific wave trait: layering, tidal timing, foam, breathing, or a bob.

6

Layered Swell

A second, fainter band crosses the first at a different angle and speed, so the two overlap and cancel unevenly instead of one uniform sheet - reads as multiple swells passing through at once, closer to real open water.

7

Tidal Pull

Same single band, re-timed with a quick surge forward and a slow drawback instead of an even back-and-forth - the asymmetry is what actually reads as "tide," since real waves don't move at a constant speed.

8

Foam-Crested Swell

A thin bright crest line rides just ahead of the swell band and fades as it passes, like a sliver of whitewater leading the wave. Most "wave-like" of the five, at the cost of a second blended layer.

9

Breathing Tide

The whole tile brightens and dims slightly in time with the swell, as if the water itself is breathing with the current - subtle, but adds a sense of volume the flat band alone doesn't have.

10

Deep Current Bob

The band drifts on a slight diagonal path instead of pure sideways, like the surface is gently rising and falling as the current passes under it. Cheapest of the five iterations - one extra transform axis, nothing more.
