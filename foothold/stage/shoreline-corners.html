Shoreline corner rounding - blended corners, 4 tunings

Follow-up to the first round: the "blended concave + convex" direction is the keeper, so these four are all variations on that one approach (round a water tile's corner where it faces land, AND round the land tile's corner where water wraps around it on two sides) - just tuned differently. Same 7×8 sample board as before, large tiles, with a diagonal staircase edge, a concave inlet, an isolated single shoal tile, and a small tide cluster elsewhere on the board, so every corner case shows up. All three tidal tile types share the treatment: ocean (permanent deep water), tide-warning (fringe about to flood, cyan ring), and tide-takeover (currently submerged, high tide).

Rough visual sketch in HTML/CSS, not the real Phaser renderer - colors match GameScene.js's OCEAN_COLOR / SHOAL_HIGH_COLOR / SHOAL_WARN_COLOR constants exactly. Nothing here is wired into the game yet.

Reference

What ships today

Every tile is a plain square, ocean and land alike - the boundary reads as a hard grid line. Kept here for a direct before/after against the four tunings.

C1

Baseline blended radius (12px)

The version from the first round, unchanged: a fixed 12px radius applied to both the water tile's exposed corners (convex) and the land tile's wrapped corners (concave), so the whole boundary - including the diagonal staircase and the inlet - reads as one continuous curve. This is the anchor the other three tunings are measured against.

C2

Bigger sweep (20px)

Same blended logic, radius pushed to 20px. The curve is much more obviously a coastline rather than a rounded grid - but on a single tile that big a radius starts eating into space a resource icon or income label would otherwise sit in, so this only really works if the corner rounding stays a background/fill effect and never has to share a tile with on-tile UI.

C3

+ wet-sand transition ring

Same blended 14px corners as the baseline, plus a thin sand-colored line traced along the exact boundary curve - both on the water tile's exposed sides and the land tile's wrapped corners - so there's a visible "wet sand" transition band between land and water, not just a color change with no in-between. Closest to a literal beach.

C4

Adaptive radius by exposure

Still the same blended convex+concave rule, but the radius itself varies per tile based on how many of its 4 orthogonal neighbors are also water: a tile surrounded on 3 sides (barely poking out) gets a small, subtle nick (8px); a typical edge tile gets the baseline (13px); an isolated tile or a sharp tip (0-1 water neighbors) gets a big, soft round (19px) so it reads as a little rounded islet instead of a sharp square. The intent is a coastline that looks hand-tuned - tight where the shore is dense, soft where it's a lone outcrop - without hand-placing a single radius value.

Implementation notes

All four are the same Phaser cost - every tuning still boils down to fillRoundedRect(x, y, w, h, {tl, tr, bl, br}) per tile (already used in GameScene.js's tile-control bar), just with different radius values or an extra thin stroke. Nothing here needs a new rendering system.

C4's adaptive radius is a few extra lines (count water neighbors, pick a radius bucket) but no new concept - reuses the same neighbor-flag check the corner logic already does.

C3's sand ring is a second strokeRoundedRect pass at the same corner radii, so it stays perfectly aligned to the curve with no extra math.
