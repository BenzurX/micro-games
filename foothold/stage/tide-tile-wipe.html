Tide Tile Wipe - Per-Tile Crossing Styles

The cascade timing (bottom-left cluster leads, top-right joins 450ms later, 240ms/step) is locked in. This is about what happens WITHIN each tile as the flood reaches it - instead of a tile flipping instantly from dry sand to flooded, the wipe visibly crosses the tile's own surface, in the same diagonal direction the flood is traveling (bottom-left cluster wipes bottom-left→top-right per tile; top-right cluster wipes top-right→bottom-left per tile). Left: one tile zoomed in on a loop, with an arrow showing the wipe direction. Right: the same style applied across the full cluster at the real cascade pace.

A - Hard diagonal edge
B - Foam-crest edge
C - Soft gradient blend
D - Corner bloom
A - Hard diagonal edge. A crisp straight line sweeps across the tile at a 45° angle, sand on one side and flooded color on the other - no blending, just a clean line in motion. Reads as the most "graphic," least organic of the four; cheapest to draw (one clip + one fill).

Full cluster, real cascade pace
B - Foam-crest edge. Same diagonal wipe as A, but the leading edge carries a bright, slightly-wider foam-colored line that fades out just behind it as the flood color fills in - reads as an actual small wave crossing the tile rather than a flat color swap.

Full cluster, real cascade pace
C - Soft gradient blend. Instead of a hard line, a soft-edged band (~35% of the tile's width) sweeps across with the two colors blending smoothly at its edges - no crisp line at all, more like water visibly soaking across the sand than a wipe transition. Gentlest, most "watery" of the four.

Full cluster, real cascade pace
D - Corner bloom. Instead of a straight line, the flood grows from the tile's own near corner (the corner facing the direction the wave is coming from) as an expanding quarter-circle, reaching the tile's far corner last. Reads as water pooling in from that corner rather than a wave washing straight across - a softer, more organic motion than a straight-line wipe.
