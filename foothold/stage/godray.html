Pseudo god rays - rainbow options

Four takes on the "rainbow light shafts" effect, layered under the DLSS sparkles. Each preview runs real Phaser objects over a faux board (additive-blended gradient beams, animated live) so whatever you pick lifts straight into src/lib/ui.js next to startDlssSparkles(). Tell me the letter and I'll wire it in behind the game like the sparkles. The faint DLSS stars are drawn on top here so you can judge how the two read together.

A Diagonal Shafts

A handful of soft rainbow beams tilted from the top-left, slowly drifting sideways and breathing in brightness. The classic "light through a window" read.

B Radial Sunburst

Rays fanning out from a single point above the top edge, gently pulsing. Reads like sun breaking through - the most focal / dramatic.

C Aurora Curtain

Tall vertical rainbow shafts that sway and swell like an aurora. Ambient, dreamy, spread evenly across the screen - least directional.

D Sweeping Prism Fan

A tight ordered spectrum fan anchored off the top-right corner, slowly rotating as one group so the colors sweep across the board. The most overtly "prismatic".

How they differ. A is directional and calm; B radiates from one hot point; C is vertical/ambient with no clear source; D is an ordered spectrum that sweeps. All use additive blending so they glow over the dark board and never fully hide it, and all are built to sit at a low depth (behind the game, above the background) exactly like the sparkles.

Tuning. Beam count, width, opacity (kept low on purpose so gameplay stays readable), sway speed, and the palette are all easy to dial after you pick - e.g. "B, but softer and slower".
