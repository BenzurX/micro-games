# Tackle First

- [x] **Turn order fix** - Balance fix: first-mover imbalance - player 1 (always moves first each round) was winning 66.8% vs player 2's 33.1% over 5000 AI-vs-AI games. Considered alternating who moves first, but that would read as "the enemy went twice in a row" at the round boundary - kept turn order fixed and compensated player 2 with a starting-resource bump instead (`P2_START_BONUS = { wood: 14, gold: 14, stone: 7 }`, tuned against the harness). Invisible to the player (AI's stockpile is never shown in the UI). Re-ran at 20,000 games: 50.7%/49.3%.
- [ ] **Mobile tooltip** - Mobile tooltip preview problem - the tooltip is wired to `pointerover`/`pointerout` (`showTooltip`/`hideTooltip` in GameScene.js), a hover state that doesn't really exist on touch. On desktop you hover-then-click, so the preview always comes before the commit; on mobile, `pointerdown` fires the move immediately - there's no preview step, so new players can't learn "what does this cost / what do I gain" before tapping. Candidate solves (need a decision, changes the touch interaction model): 1. Tap-to-select / tap-again-to-confirm: first tap on an actionable tile shows the tooltip only; a second tap on the same tile commits. Closest to how most mobile strategy games solve this, but is a bigger interaction change.
- [x] **PWA support** - PWA build for the web version - `manifest.webmanifest` + `sw.js` (cache-first service worker, offline + installable), wired into `index.html`, verified in-browser (SW registers/activates, cache holds all 24 precached files). Icons are a temporary placeholder (upscaled `watchtower.png`) - swap to the real files once the custom app icon task is done. CLAUDE.md's pre-push gate now has the `CACHE_VERSION` bump step.
- [x] **Total Victory** - Total Victory (fun polish) - capturing the enemy base while owning every non-river tile and having every held node upgraded now triggers "Complete Foothold! / Round N" / "The enemy has nothing left", a gold sunburst (pulsing ADD-blend glow + 10 rotating rays, sized off the canvas diagonal so it reaches the screen edges in both portrait and wide layouts), a camera flash/shake, and a unique "Grand Finale" fanfare (`sfx.js`: horn call, rising flourish, sustained chord, shimmering bells). Staged 10 fanfare candidates + 3 flare variants in `stage/total-victory.html` before picking. Tuned glow brightness down twice after playtest (alpha 1 -> 0.55 -> 0.28) since the ADD blend stacked much brighter than the mockup.



# Future Ideas

Ideas for future maps/levels, each its own board with its own terrain gimmick - not reskins of the river, and not required to keep a river at all (the river is just level 1's terrain, other levels can drop it or replace it with something else entirely):
- [ ] **Mountains level** - Mountains - a SOLID blocking terrain, zero crossings anywhere. Splits the board into disconnected pockets, so the opening becomes "which pocket did I land in" rather than a symmetric two-halves map. Bottleneck tactics come from there being no crossing at all, not from defending one. How would the player achieve victory in a level like this?
- [ ] **Fire/hell level** - Fire/hell level - hazard tiles (lava) that tax whoever holds them: an owned hazard tile periodically burns a bit of stockpile instead of producing income, so it's a high-risk/high-reward land grab (probably paired with a bonus resource on/near it) rather than free territory. The high risk or burn territories should have a visual flame indicator when it's burning your resources along with a unique sound.
- [ ] **Forest level** - Forest level - fog of war: enemy tiles/nodes outside your vision radius are hidden until a tile of yours is adjacent to them. Changes the game from perfect-information to scouting-driven; the AI would need vision logic added too, not just new art. Visual direction picked: "C - Foliage Canopy" fog treatment staged in `stage/forest-fog.html` - a dark mossy forest-floor base under two slow-drifting grey-white mist layers, painted once across the whole board (not per-tile) so canopy blobs and mist banks read as continuous. Priciest of the 3 staged options to build well for real (would want a proper Aseprite/painted fog texture + sprite animation instead of CSS gradients). Reveal holes are soft-edged/blurred so newly-scouted ground fades in.
- [ ] **Ocean level** - Ocean level - water covers most of the board and crossings (bridges, or a new build-a-bridge action) are scarce/expensive, so crossings themselves become the thing players fight over, instead of the open land they currently fight over on a river map. Limited/scarce resources on islands that you build between
